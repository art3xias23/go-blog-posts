Today I was travelling away from home.


As usual, I packed a bag with my keyboard, laptop, hub and mouse. I didn't get to open the bag on my arrival


In the morning I had some business to tend to prior to me starting my workday


By the time I arrived home after my errands I saw I had only a minute before my morning stand up with my team


As I was pulling my charger from my bag it had unfortunatelly tangled up with my keyboard's usb-c cable.


The usb port on the left side of my keyboard, which is the master side was no longer working


Funnily, connecting the cable to the right side was working, however the sides were mirrored. Nevertheless, that told me that the issue was in the usb port and not the Elite-C microcontroller


Took me a couple of hours but I finally ended up fixing my issue


I am on a Windows 10 machine and I followed the qmk guide: https://docs.qmk.fm/newbs_getting_started


  1. Downloaded qmk msys.

  2. Ran qmk setup.

  3. Generated my own set of config.h and keymap.c files by following the guide on the qmk website

  4. Previously I used Layout in order to generate a .JSON and a .hex file. So now I used this to convert my pre-existing JSON to a .c file (old_json_file.c)

  5. Replace the new keymap.c file contents with the contents from old_json_file.c

  6. As suggested in config.h I commented out

    7. // #define MASTER_LEFT

    and uncommented

    #define MASTER_RIGHT

  7. I ran qmk compile which generated a new .hex file for me

  8. Opened qmk toolbox. Opened the hex file inside it. Inserted the usb c cable into the right keyboard. Hit the reset button and viola.

  9. Just to confirm I did not need to flash the left side, flashing only the right side got both halves working. (edited)
